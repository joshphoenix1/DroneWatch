IF ALERT IS SEVERE OR EXTREME

Move now. Go to shelter, a basement, stairwell, corridor, or interior room. Stay away from windows, balconies, roofs, and exterior walls.

If you hear a drone, blast, siren, or official warning, do not wait for the dashboard to be perfect. Protect yourself first.

HOW WE KNOW

The MVP checks public CAP alert feeds, configured OSINT feeds, and local vetted reports for the selected region. Every alert shows source, confidence, age, and whether the source check is healthy.

It works when a trusted source publishes fast enough and the backend can reach it. It cannot guarantee detection of every small drone or munition.

THREAT LEVELS

EXTREMEOfficial or highly corroborated immediate danger. Shelter now.
SEVERECredible inbound or nearby attack warning. Get away from glass and exposed rooms.
MODERATEPossible or unverified threat in the broader area. Prepare shelter and keep checking official alerts.
MINORAdvisory or context. No immediate matching threat in the configured area.

CONFIDENCE AND FRESHNESS

Official means a recognized alert source. OSINT means a vetted report or feed, not authority. Demo means synthetic test data only.

Fresh alerts are recent. Stale alerts are older than 15 minutes or missing timestamps. Stale does not mean safe, but it means verify elsewhere.

MAKE IT WORK IN REAL LIFE

Keep official civil-defense alerts enabled. Add verified local report sources. Test delivery weekly. Do not rely on one feed, one server, or one network.

The current 3D basemap is the Lebanon prototype view. Region switching changes alert feeds and filtering; future map tiles should reload per region.

COMMUNITY CONFIRMATION

If a report matches what you personally saw or heard, confirm it here. Do not move toward danger or go outside to check.
